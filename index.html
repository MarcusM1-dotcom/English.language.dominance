How English Took Over the World

And What It’s Cost Us

Intro Rise Modern Cost Conclusion

Introduction

Imagine you invent something amazing—but no one hears about it because it’s not in English.

English is more than a language. It’s power, access, and sometimes a barrier.

The Rise of English

English spread because of power, not because it was easier.

  • The British Empire controlled about 24% of the world.
  • In India, English became the language of education.
  • Nigeria has over 500 languages, but English is official.
  • Speaking English often meant better jobs and status.

This is called linguistic imperialism.

The Modern World

  • 80–90% of scientific journals are in English
  • Non-English research is cited less
  • 95% of top universities teach in English
  • About half of internet content is English

English acts as a global gatekeeper.

English Today

English is now a lingua franca—a shared global language.

  • Indian English
  • Nigerian English
  • Online simplified English

People adapt English to fit their cultures.

The Hidden Cost

  • 40% of languages are endangered
  • One language disappears every two weeks
  • Languages carry cultural and scientific knowledge

This is called cultural erosion.

Language Shapes Thought

Language affects how we think.

Some languages use directions instead of left/right, making speakers better at navigation.

Conclusion

English helps us connect, but it also creates inequality.

The goal is balance—keeping global communication while protecting languages.
